NoteTaker

Taking notes while paying full attention in class can be challenging.

NoteTaker is an app where a student can record a lecture, and write down notes simultaneously. Once the lecture is played back, the student can click on the notes they wrote, and track when they wrote them during the lecture. A student can also turn on the speech-to-text editor where it converts the lecturer's speech and saves it as text in the student's personal page.

Technologies: Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, jQuery, AJAX, AWS S3, recorder.js, and HTML5 Audio.

SplitMate

An app that gamifies splitting chores among roommates.

Roommates can assign chores to themself, or get auto-assigned based off of their chores' history. Every chore completed earns points. Roommates can use points to delegate chores to other roommates. If a chore is incompleted before the deadline, the roommate has to pay a penalty fee. Also, every chore gets voted by roommates. If the majority agrees or disagrees with the completed chore then that roommate gets rewarded or penalized for that chore.

Technologies: Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, AJAX, JavaScript, CSS (Foundation), APIs (Google Street View), ActionMailer, RSpec, and jQuery UI.

Common Fibers

Common Fibers is a group-buying retail site that connects manufacturers and consumers. Here's how it works:

Manufacturers post a pair of jeans, with an item limit of 2000 pairs. That means that the jeans won't be shipped until all 2000 pairs are pre-sold. Customers can buy the jeans at a low price, and will get notified once all the jeans are bought. The pair of jeans that the customer pre-ordered will now be shipped to them.

Technologies: Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, CSS, and Action Mailer.
